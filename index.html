Parliament and Voting

The Parliament of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club (a.k.a. Hack Club Parliament) is the primary legislative body representing the Democratic Republic of Hack Club. To ensure fair representative democracy, every 2-3 months, the Election Oversight Committee (H.C.E.C.) will host General Elections where all citizens of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club (including non-residential citizens) can partake, which will decide the distribution of Parliament seats.

Seatholders of the Hack Club Parliament are allowed to draft and vote on bills, where if they are valid and legal, shall be appended to the Legislation or Constitution of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club.

Voter ID

Voter IDs protect our democracy by ensuring all ballots casted are legitimate and real. Before casting votes national votes, citizens are required to prove their identity by obtaining a 6-character alphanumeric Voter ID after signing in with their Slack account, HCA account, or Non-residential citizenship credentials. This prevents malicious actors from voting on behalf of others during elections.

National-level Voting

All citizens are allowed to vote on the national level. There are two main types of votes citizens can partake in, the General Election, and referendums.

General Elections occurs every 2-3 months and usually lasts around seven days, and decides the seat distribution of the following Parliament. General Election ballots are always ranked choice to ensure the fairest representation unlike alternative voting methods like First Past The Post (FPTP). Learn more here .

Referendums let citizens to directly vote on bills, and whether they pass to append to the Legislation or Constitution. Each vote here counts the same regardless of seat possession. All Constitutional Amendments must go through a nationwide referendum after passing through the Parliament.

Election Oversight Committee

National-level elections are managed and reviewed by the Hack Club Election Committee (H.C.E.C.) which operates as the main Oversight committee for elections within the Democratic Republic of Hack Club. Members of the Election Oversight Committee are not allowed to run for national-level office, nor promote in official capacity for any party. They help ensure your ballot is secure and work with the Department of Software Engineering and Maintenance (DoSEM) to create and prevent fraud and malicious influence on elections.

Contact HCEC at hcec@hcgov.uk.

Citizenship and Immigration

All citizens of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club are given the rights and protections stated in the Constitution and Legislation of the nation. Anyone who is a full member of Hack Club, including alumni, as well as Non-residential Citizens are considered citizens of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club. The National Immigration and Citizenship Office (NIACO) overlooks citizenship and immigration such as Non-residential citizenship applications.

You can contact NIACO at:
niaco@hcgov.uk for general inquiries
immigration@hcgov.uk for immigration related inquiries
citizenship@hcgov.uk for citizenship related inquiries

Non-residential Citizenship

Non-residential Citizenship allows for people who are not on the Hack Club Slack or HCA account, such as those who were deactivated, to stay or become a citizen of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club.


Court Representation and Legal

By the Constitution of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club, all citizens and individuals within the Democratic Republic of Hack Club are given the right to due process, and shall not be detained, imprisoned, and judicially punished without the due process of law. Any citizen can request to press charges with the Court. The Supreme Court of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club is the highest Court in the nation and has the role of upholding the Constitution - the basis and foundation of the laws of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club - and the rights and protections given by it.

National Attorney Office

The Hack Club National Attorney Office (HCNAO) is responsible for defending and representing the Government of the Democratic Republic of Hack Club in the court of law. The office is led by the Lead National Attorney.

Government Services Engineering & Maintenance

The Democratic Republic of Hack Club Government relies on many digital systems to fulfil its many services and duties. The Government body managing this is the Department of Software Engineering and Maintenance (DoSEM). DoSEM is a fully publicly open agency which serves to create, upgrade, and maintain digital infrastructure for other Government agencies and functions. This means you and anyone else can join DoSEM and contribute to the Government's digital infrastructure.

The Government's digital infrastructure is open source and public on GitHub. Any citizen can contribute to any Government repository by forking and submitting a pull request.

FAQ and Resources

I need support, but I am not in the Hack Club Slack.
You can join the Democratic Republic of Hack Club Discord Embassy here, or email the relevant Government entity. Please note that the embassy is only open to Hack Club alumni and those below 19 years old as per the Hack Club Code of Conduct.
I died, what do I do?
You can write a Petition of Writ of Restoration to the Supreme Court, stating how you died, and who caused your death. Once your petition is filed, the Supreme Court must hear it within 24 hours, and you will go to trial against your killer. If the killer has no valid basis for your death, the Court will issue a Writ of Restoration, allowing you to come back to life.
How do I get married?
You can apply for a Marriage Certificate with the Supreme Court by filing an application listing your partner(s), the date you are getting married, and the location of marriage.
How do I exit out of a marriage?
You can apply to exit a marriage with the Supreme Court by filling out an application listing the partner(s) you want to exit the marriage with, the reason why, the date, and the location of marriage.
What I need is not listed here.
You can ask Government staff on Slack, the embassy, or email the relevant department. You can scroll down for a list of contacts and platforms.
